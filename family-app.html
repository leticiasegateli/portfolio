Healthcare Aged Care Product Design

Redesigning How Families Monitor Daily Care

Transforming complex sensor data into a clear, reassuring experience for family caregivers of older adults.

Context

Supporting Independence, Quietly

This project builds on CSIRO research into sensor-based systems for tracking Activities of Daily Living (ADLs) for older adults living independently or in care. Using non-invasive sensors, the system monitors movement, sleep patterns, meal routines, and room-to-room activity — helping identify early signs of health decline before they become crises.

The technology is powerful. But the interface presenting this data to families? Difficult to understand, overwhelming to parse, and easy to ignore. This project focuses entirely on the human side: making the insights clear, accessible, and actionable.

Problem

Data Without Meaning Creates Anxiety, Not Confidence

Family caregivers struggled to interpret sensor outputs from ADL monitoring systems. The result: difficulty confidently assessing their loved one's wellbeing, and uncertainty about when or how to act.

Problem 1

Too technical

Information was dense and required interpretation most family members couldn't easily do.

Problem 2

No clear normal

There was no indication of what was expected behaviour vs something worth worrying about.

Problem 3

Changes invisible

Important shifts in routine weren't surfaced. You had to know what to look for.

Problem 4

No emotional design

The interface provided no reassurance — the emotional core of what caregivers actually need.

Goal

Early Intervention, Not Dashboards

The goal is early intervention and better care outcomes — not dashboards of raw sensor data.

Design · Decisions

From Sensor Output to Human Insight

The design translates passive sensor data into three core screens: a daily status overview, an activity timeline, and a smart alerts feed — each built around minimising cognitive load on the caregiver.

Before

Dense, technical data — no hierarchy or context

No indication of normal vs concerning behaviour

Changes invisible — you had to know what to look for

No emotional design or reassurance for caregivers

Existing App CSIRO, 2022

Family portal allowing family members to view client's data, created by CSIRO (Zhang et al., 2022)

After

✓

Clear daily status card with human language

✓

Visual cues distinguishing routine from deviation

✓

Smart alerts only for genuine changes

✓

Warm, reassuring tone throughout

01 / 07

Home — Sensor Stats

The home screen gives caregivers an at-a-glance view of sensor data. Fall alerts, call frequency, door activity and meal prep are surfaced as cards with plain-language summaries.

02 / 07

Quick Actions

A bottom sheet with key actions — download reports, set travel mode, add notes, or report a sensor fault — keeping high-value tasks one tap away.

03 / 07

Insights & Notifications

AI-generated insights are surfaced at the top of the home screen. Positive changes like improved mobility are highlighted to reassure caregivers, not just flag problems.

04 / 07

Wellness Score

A daily wellness score breaks down Transfer, Self-Care and Mobility into simple percentages with trend indicators — making complex ADL data instantly readable.

05 / 07

Smart Care — AI Assistant

Caregivers can ask natural-language questions about their loved one's data. The AI assistant answers questions like 'Did Mum skip any meals?' or 'Was Mom more active than usual?'.

06 / 07

Timeline

A chronological daily timeline shows every sensor event — bedroom, toilet, dining room, shower — giving caregivers full visibility without overwhelming them.

07 / 07

Fall Alert

When a fall is detected, caregivers are immediately prompted to call or mark it as a false alarm. Clear action paths reduce panic and ensure fast response.

Impact

Confidence Over Complexity

The redesign shifts the experience from data literacy to emotional clarity — giving caregivers the confidence to know things are okay, and the clarity to act when they're not.
